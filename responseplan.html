Response plan

Introduction

Australia faced severe bushfires for a decade. Response plan is what measures needed to be taken during , before and after bushfires.

Before Bushfire

Get a backup plan when you're unable to wake up early. Being armed for backup plans for various situations will help keep you safe and improve your chances of success. You must provide backup arrangements if you are unable to evacuate as scheduled, avoid the execution of your primary fire plan. Do let your family and friends know about your decisions and goals to stay or move.

Detail

During bushfire

Keep up-to-date using more than one set of knowledge so you'll know if a fire has started near you. This may include a battery-powered phone, chatting to the neighbors, and finding other local knowledge. Any fires could start so easily that they could destroy homes and life in a matter of minutes. Don't wait for an alert to execute your strategy for the protection of the forest fire. Taking precautions are better than failing.

Detail

After bushfires

If you've left, don't go back home until you know it's safe to do so. For those that have remained and protected their houses, keep wearing your protective gear and watch your land for a few hours after the fire front has gone. It's going to be dangerous because the smoke from burning products can be poisonous. Make sure to drink lot of water to stay hydrated and evacuate to the nearest safe zones.

Bushfire recovery PWC

All of us at PwC Australia have been shocked and devastated by the bushfire crisis, and grieve for the communities who have been directly impacted. We are committed to rebuilding these communities, and to supporting the brave volunteers doing all they can to help. As such, we have donated $500K to the Australian Red Cross and implemented uncapped paid leave for our people who are volunteering with our emergency services.

Bushfire Threat

Precautions and definitions to fight bushfire and stay safe.

• Bushfire is an unplanned fire primarily in vegetation such as grass, forest and scrub • Bushfire consequences are the impact of the bushfire on: o people and the social, community and health environment o the economic environment o the built environment o the natural environment. • Prevention is the elimination or reduction of the incidence or severity of emergencies and the mitigation of their effects. • Response is the combating of emergencies and the provision of rescue and immediate relief services. • Recovery is the assisting of people and communities affected by emergencies to achieve a proper and effective level of functioning.
